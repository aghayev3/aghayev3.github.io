What I Do

Blue team work, quiet mornings, loud alerts.

My day-to-day is SOC monitoring, detection engineering, and incident response. I write Sigma rules, dig through forensic images, automate Active Directory environments, and build tools that make defenders' lives easier.

As team lead for Team AURORA, I built IDRIS — a self-hosted identity threat detection & response platform that covers the full AD kill-chain: Kerberoasting, DCSync, Golden Tickets, the works. It earned commendation from programme instructors for its depth and completeness.

I believe good security work looks a lot like good engineering — methodical, documented, and boring enough that nothing exciting ever happens on your watch.

Projects

IDRIS — Identity Detection & Response Intelligence System

Capstone / Team Lead / Instructor Commended

The big one. Self-hosted ITDR platform built as the Cohort 7 capstone. As team lead, I designed the architecture and coordinated the build from threat emulation through auto-remediation. Covers the full on-prem AD identity kill-chain: Kerberoasting, AS-REP roasting, DCSync, pass-the-hash, Golden/Silver tickets — validated in real time against Wazuh.

Ships a 30-rule Sigma detection library mapped to MITRE ATT&CK, BloodHound-style attack-path visualization, per-identity risk scoring, AI-generated remediation, and a closed detect-fix-retest loop.

stack: Active Directory, Wazuh, Sigma, MITRE ATT&CK, BloodHound, Python, PowerShell

Windows DFIR Investigation

Enterprise Compromise Analysis / AzCC Technion / 2026

Full-scope forensic investigation across two disk images (Win10 + Server 2022 DC). Reconstructed a 5-phase attack chain: WhatsApp ClickFix foothold, DACL persistence, Mimikatz recon, WinRM lateral movement, and a 46-second cleanup. Delivered a 25-page report with timeline and IOC list.

tools: Autopsy, FTK Imager, Registry Explorer, Eric Zimmerman Tools
